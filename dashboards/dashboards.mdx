Dashboards give you a bird's-eye view of your workspace. Instead of digging through individual lists to understand how work is going, a dashboard surfaces the answers immediately, as charts you can read in seconds.

## Create a dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Dashboards">Click **Dashboards** in the sidebar.</Step>
  <Step title="Create a new dashboard">
    Click **+ New dashboard** and give it a name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add charts">
    Click **+ Add chart** and choose what you want to track.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What charts can show

- Tasks completed vs. remaining
- Tasks grouped by status or priority
- Workload by assignee (who has the most tasks open?)
- Tasks created over time (is work growing faster than it's being completed?)

## Useful dashboard setups

- **Sprint health:** completed vs. remaining tasks for the current sprint
- **Team workload:** task counts by assignee to spot imbalances before they cause problems
- **Project status:** tasks by status for a specific list, updated in real time

<Tip>
  Dashboards are workspace-specific. Share the same dashboard with your whole
  team so everyone has the same view of progress when planning or reviewing.
</Tip>
