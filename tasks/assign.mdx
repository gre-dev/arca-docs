Assigning a task removes ambiguity. When every task has an owner, your team knows exactly who is responsible and nothing slips through the cracks. An unassigned task is an ownerless task, and ownerless tasks don't get done.

## How to assign a task

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the task">
    Click the task title to open the task detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the Assignees field">
    Find the **Assignees** field in the right-hand sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select one or more teammates">
    Choose people from the dropdown. You can assign multiple people to a task if
    needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for effective assignments

- **One primary owner:** even when multiple people contribute, designate one person as the main responsible party. Shared ownership often means nobody owns it.
- **Assign before the meeting ends:** if a task comes out of a discussion, assign it immediately while everyone's attention is on it.
- **Check for unassigned tasks regularly:** periodically scan your lists for tasks without assignees and make sure each one has a clear owner.
- **Filter views by assignee:** create a view filtered to your name to see exactly what's on your plate for the day or week.

<Note>If someone doesn't appear in the assignee list, they're not a member of the workspace yet. Invite them from **Settings > Members**.</Note>

Assigned users are automatically subscribed to the task and receive notifications when it's updated, commented on, or has its status changed.
